About

Based on the Dawson-Nielsen construction (arXiv:quant-ph/0505030). The visualization shows a schematic of the recursive approximation near the identity in \(\SU{2}\).

The Solovay-Kitaev Theorem. The Solovay-Kitaev theorem shows that any gate set generating a dense subgroup of \(\SU{2}\) can approximate any unitary to accuracy \(\varepsilon\) using \(O(\log^{3.97}(1/\varepsilon))\) gates. The algorithm decomposes the residual error as a group commutator \(VWV^\dagger W^\dagger\), where \(V\) and \(W\) are each \(\sqrt{\varepsilon}\)-close to identity. Recursive approximation of \(V\) and \(W\) yields \(\varepsilon^{3/2}\) convergence per level.
